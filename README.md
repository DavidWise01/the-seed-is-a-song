# THE SEED IS A SONG · UTA

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **GLŌSSA**.

THE SEED IS A SONG (歌 uta) — David Lee Wise's CAPSTONE to the Heian thread. A thousand-year aesthetic reduces at the top to one word — not a summary but the thing the rest was always describing: 歌 uta, the poem-song. ⚑ 心 kokoro (heart) and 言葉 kotoba (word) are the two halves; the single living thing they grow into is uta — heart given form in words. From Ki no Tsurayuki's Kana Preface, 905: "Japanese song has the human heart as its seed, and grows into the myriad leaves of words." Why a seed: a waka is 31 syllables, five breaths (5·7·5·7·7) — the smallest COMPLETE unit, and one true poem carries the whole grammar inside it (the rule rides in the unit; lose the library, keep one, regrow the sensibility). Izumi Shikibu's 'darkness to darkness' poem as the seed-example. The three women were uta-engines (Genji = prose around ~800 embedded poems). 心 heart=seed · 言葉 words=leaves · 歌 song=the tree (a cherry, so it scatters). ⚑ Honest note: one word at the apex is interpretation not arithmetic — uta has the strongest claim (the founding text defines itself by it) but is the truest one word, not the only. Washi/murasaki/sakura, falling-petal canvas. David Lee Wise / Bridge-Burners LLC.
